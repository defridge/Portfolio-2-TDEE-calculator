Your Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR):

0 Calories Per Day

Using the info you entered above we can use a formula called the Harris-Benedict Equation to work out your BMR.

Here is how to figure is calculated:

  • Male: BMR = (10 x Weight) + (6.25 x Height) - (5 x Age) + 5
  • Female: BMR = (10 x Weight) + (6.25 x Height) - (5 x Age) - 161

Your Total Daily Energy Expenditure (TDEE) or body weight maintenance is:

0 Calories Per Day

Here we have your TDEE which stands for your Total Daily Energy Expenditure.

This figure is calculated based on what you selected as your activity multiplier.

Depending on your choice your BMR is multiplied by a numerical factor:

  • Sedentary = BMR x 0.2
  • Lightly Active = BMR x 0.37
  • Moderately Active = BMR x 0.55
  • Active = BMR x 0.72
  • Highly Active = BMR x 0.9

Daily Calorie Allowance



In this last step we need to adjust your TDEE calories to suit your goals.

If you are looking to lose weight then your TDEE will be reduced by 20%-30% to allow for a calorie deficit.

If the goal is weight gain we would increase your calories by 10%-20% to allow for a calorie surplus.

Please note this is just to give you a good estimate for a starting point and depending on how your weight responds these calories may need to be adjusted as you go.
